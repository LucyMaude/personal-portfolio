Lucy Maude is a free-lance web developer. She specializes in taking clients’ visions and turning them into beautifully crafted websites. Her experience in customer service and skill in development help her cultivate relationships with clients, realize their goals, and deliver products in a timely, results-driven manner.

Lucy first discovered her passion for tech while at the University of Richmond. For all four years, she worked at the University’s Technology Learning Center where she learned the ins-and-outs of a variety of software and taught students, faculty, and staff to use these programs. After graduating, she worked in Virginia for 2 years before deciding to chase her interest in development. In 2017 she and her husband packed their car and drove to Salt Lake City to attend VSchool, a web development boot camp. After completing the program, she started her own business designing and developing websites.

Lucy loves to try new things. Her personal motto is: “Do not go where the path may lead. Go instead where there is no path and leave a trail” (Ralph Waldo Emerson). This has led her to live in Santiago Chile, join Frisbee and lacrosse teams, and learn sewing and Logic Pro (among many other things). She also has a knack for teaching and leadership; instructing Spanish and music classes while over-seas and leading meetings for her volunteer organizations from the time she was in High School.

If you are interested in working with me, please contact me and we can discuss your vision/opportunity.
